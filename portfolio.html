Data + Web

Barriers to Care
The Affordable Care Act expanded access to mental health services. But patients still have trouble seeing psychiatrists, who, doctors and advocates say, are overbooked, hard to find — and often not accepting insurance at all.
My Take: I spent a month researching mental health care, speaking to psychiatrists and advocates and analyzing data for this text-and-data story. The result, presented on a custom-built site featuring Highcharts, Chart.js, Carto and jQuery graphics, shows how difficult it is to access care.
A Visual Look at Our Favorite Movies
There are movies we think are good and movies we all pay to go see. These movies are not always the same. This is a visual look at our favorite movies, using IMDb ratings and data of box office ticket sales (adjusted for inflation) from Box Office Mojo.
My Take: This project features visualizations of data analysis about movies — done initially in Excel, then presented with Highcharts and Google Fusion Tables on a custom web page.
Germany's Run to World Cup Victory
The German men's national soccer team did not lose a single game on the road to winning the 2014 World Cup, their fourth title. This story — told through graphics — documents their path to victory.
My Take: This project started with an Excel analysis of soccer data and resulted in a custom site that incorporates Google Fusion Tables and Highcharts.
Explore St. Louis
Explore St. Louis is the area's official tourism site, advertising events, sightseeing spots and news about St. Louis from the Convention and Visitors Commission.
My Take: I served as lead designer on this project during my two-summer internship at Integrity, a St. Louis web development firm. I presented a user experience audit to executives, built all 16 page templates and designed the home page and visual styles throughout the site.
